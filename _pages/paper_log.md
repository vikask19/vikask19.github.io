## Blogs & Newsletters

Sources I follow and find worth reading regularly.

### ML & Deep Learning

| Source | Why I Read It |
|--------|---------------|
| [Lilian Weng's Blog](https://lilianweng.github.io) | Deep, well-cited posts on architectures, RL, and alignment. The kind of writing that ages well. |
| [Andrej Karpathy's Blog](https://karpathy.github.io) | Rare posts but high signal. The neural net hacker mindset explained clearly. |
| [Sebastian Ruder's NLP Newsletter](https://newsletter.ruder.io) | Best way to stay current on NLP research without reading every arxiv paper. |
| [Eugene Yan's Blog](https://eugeneyan.com) | ML in production — the gap between research and shipping. Very practical. |
| [Chip Huyen's Blog](https://huyenchip.com/blog/) | ML systems design, MLOps, and career stuff. Her [Designing ML Systems](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/designing-machine-learning/9781098107956/) is required reading. |

### Engineering & Systems

| Source | Why I Read It |
|--------|---------------|
| [The Morning Paper](https://blog.acolyer.org) | CS papers explained accessibly. Good for keeping up with systems research. |
| [High Scalability](http://highscalability.com) | Real-world architecture walkthroughs from companies that've scaled. |
| [Martin Fowler's Blog](https://martinfowler.com) | Software design thinking. Useful when thinking about ML system architecture. |

### Occasional Reads

| Source | Why |
|--------|-----|
| [Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com) | Noisy but occasionally surfaces something important first. |
| [ML Subreddit](https://reddit.com/r/MachineLearning) | Good for tracking what the community is excited about. |
| [The Gradient](https://thegradient.pub) | Long-form analysis of ML trends and research. Better than most journals for practical perspective. |
